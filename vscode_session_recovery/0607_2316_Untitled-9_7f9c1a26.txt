In the baseline, MHC is bounded by $\text{MSHR}_{L,size}$ of the first level accepting the demand. MARS raises this bound via a lower-level's MSHR. Among requests arriving at $L{+}1$, only those that miss---a fraction $M_{L+1}$---consume $\text{MSHR}_{L+1}$ entries; the rest hit at $L{+}1$ and return without MSHR allocation. Consequently, up to $\text{MSHR}_{(L+1,size)}/M_{L+1}$ requests from $L$ can be in flight simultaneously before the missing fraction exhausts $\text{MSHR}_{L+1}$. The upper bound $\text{MHC}_\Omega$ (Equation~\ref{eq:mhc_bound}) is therefore reached when $L$'s MSHR is fully occupied and $\text{MSHR}_{L+1}$ is fully utilized by forwarded misses, where $L$ denotes the first cache level accepting the demand and $M_{L+1}$ the miss ratio at level $L{+}1$:
